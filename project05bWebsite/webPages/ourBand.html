Meet the Musical Eggcorns

Lady Mondegreen's Consort

The Skilled Instrumentalists Who Perform With Lady Mondegreen

For several months after moving to London, Lady Mondegreen mostly performed alone. A proficient lutenist, she was able to handle solo gigs quite well, but always felt that something was missing. Occasionally, she would be asked to sit in with travelling consorts as a lutenist or backup vocalist, and those experiences always left her feeling envious of those well-established ensembles. However, one night on her way home from a tavern where she'd been performing, she ran into two childhood friends of hers: Philippa and Lettice Godfrey.

The three women had spent many happy days playing music together in their youths, and quickly picked up right where they left off. That very evening, Lady Mondegreen accompanied the Godfreys back to their living quarters, and the three of them spent the entire night singing standard madrigals. Lady Mondegreen was blown away by what accomplished lutenists both Philippa and Lettice had become, and she immediately asked whether they would be interested in performing with her as her own foundling consort.

Philippa and Lettice, excited to be founding members of a new ensemble, jumped at the chance. As well-connected instrumentalists, they helped Lady Mondegreen cobble together an increasingly large consort. First, they added Geoffrey Wolfden on bass viol. Soon afterwards, Piers Moland joined the group on soprano viol. Geoffrey and Piers invited their friends Thomasine Ginter and Diggory Good, who play crumhorn and sackbut, respectively. After hearing the ensemble live, both wholeheartedly agreed to join.

At long last, the group found a suitable percussionist. Lambert ”Lam“ Sparrow joined the consort along with his wife, Sabine, a wonderful flute and recorder player. At that point, everyone in the ensemble agreed that the project felt complete. Many of the musicians listed above are veritable instrumental chameleons who play secondary and even tertiary instruments as well, so it's almost as if we have nearly double the number of musicians we appear to have! Now, Lady Mondegreen and her Eggcorns enjoy a lively touring life, traveling all over the country to various estates, castles, and faires, performing songs they grew up with, songs they wrote themselves, and everything in-between.
